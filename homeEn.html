About Me

Born and raised in North Dakota, I grew up with a strong appreciation the arts, science, and technology.

My early university studies tipped in the direction of the arts with a bachelor's in Spanish and tipped back toward the sciences with graduate studies in Hispanic Linguistics. I dedicated my doctoral studies to the topic of collaboration in conversation and designing innovative new research methods that incorporate technology. Nevertheless, after 12 rewarding years teaching Spanish language and linguistics, I opted to pursue opportunities beyond academia. Whereas many academics struggle to sustain a livable wage under increasingly unreasonable teaching and service obligations, I could not bring myself to capitalize on educating future generations of academics for a excessively saturated job market.

My IT expertise began in my teenage years, troubleshooting hardware and operating systems during high school. At 13, I used MS-DOS to locate executable files and play video games without my parents' permission. By 17, I worked full time as a technical support representative for Gateway Computers and SBC Global broadband services. I initially self-educated in web development and content design for leisure; however, my research required the use of script interpreting programs as well. In my new pursuit of software development, I apply my extensive linguistic understanding of human languages to programming languages as well.

My overall vision is to integrate my background and experience in language and linguistics with my future in software development.

My current trajectory at Eleven Fifty Academy is to become a full stack JavaScript developer in Node.js, React, and Angular.

In five years, I foresee myself pursuing means of integrating machine learning into software development.
